What is a CPF (Carbon Pipe Filter?)

Our design is an easy-to-make, affordable, simple water filter than effectively cleans harmful particles and chemicals out of the water.

How does our filter work?

Our filter uses activated carbon to filter potentially harmful carbon from the water. Chemicals and particles residing in water that is consumed by a human being can pose serious health risks and potentially be deadly.

Charcoal is a form of activated carbon. Charcoal is an inexpensive and easily obtainable item. It can be created by burning and heating up wood. Our filter uses charcoal to clear the water of chemicals.

Gravel and pebbles are used to filter out larger particles, such as wood chunks, leaves, and so on.

Sand is then used to filter out smaller, finer particles. It also can filter out small organisms, such as bacteria.

How is it assembled?

These materials should go in a order like the following:

1 (top layer): Gravel

2:Sand

3:Carbon

4:Sand

5:Carbon

They should be placed within a tube. For example: a tin can, plumbing pipe, water bottle. At the bottom of the tube should be one (or several) small opening. It can be puched out with a pen/pencil tip, toothpick, thumbtack, sharp stick, or knife tip.

The interior (not to scale) diagram

Is there anything unique about our model?

This model has differences to the already existing filters. It uses an extra layer of sand and carbon to improve performance. It also uses a small opening hole(s) instead of cloth to prevent the contents of the filter from falling out. Cloth can be dirty, and thus the water is less clean and contaminated once again as it passes through it.

The inside materials that make up the filter can be put into a variety of materials, preferably in a tin can, water bottle, or a pipe-or-tube-like container. Each layer should be roughly the same thickness (each layer is 1 inch thick minimum).
